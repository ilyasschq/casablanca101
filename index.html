Hassan II Mosque

Hassan II Mosque

The Hassan II Mosque is the largest mosque in Africa and the 3rd largest in the world. Located partly on the Atlantic Ocean, its 210-meter minaret is the world's tallest. This architectural masterpiece can accommodate 105,000 worshippers and features exquisite Moroccan craftsmanship.

Visiting Hours

Summer (April 1 - Sept 30): 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM
Winter (Oct 1 - March 31): 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
Closed on Fridays for non-Muslim visitors

Admission Fees

Adults: 130 MAD
Children (under 12): 30 MAD
Students (with ID): 30 MAD
Moroccan Residents: 60 MAD

Tour Information

• Guided tours only (mandatory)
• Tour duration: ~1 hour
• Languages: Arabic, French, English, Spanish
• Last admission: 1 hour before closing

Tour Includes

• Prayer Hall (main hall)
• Ablution Room
• Hammam (traditional bath)
• Museum
• Exterior areas and gardens

Important Notes

• Dress code: Modest clothing required (shoulders and knees covered)
• Photography: Allowed except during prayer times
• Accessibility: Wheelchair accessible with ramps and elevators
• Reservations: Not required for individuals; groups should book in advance

Highlights

• Retractable roof weighing 1,100 tons
• Glass floor section with view to sea below
• Laser beam from minaret pointing toward Mecca
• Heated floors in the prayer hall
• Materials: Marble from Agadir, cedar wood from Middle Atlas, granite from Tafraoute

Location & Contact

• Address: Boulevard Sidi Mohammed Ben Abdallah, Casablanca
• Construction: 1986-1993 (7 years)
• Capacity: 25,000 indoors, 80,000 outdoors
• Architect: Michel Pinseau
• Workforce: 10,000 artisans worked on the project

Old Medina of Casablanca

Old Medina

The Old Medina of Casablanca is the historic heart of the city, dating back to the 16th century. Unlike other Moroccan medinas, it's relatively small but packed with authentic shops, traditional crafts, and local life. Wander through its narrow alleys to discover hidden gems.

What to Buy

Leather goods, pottery, traditional clothing, spices, and souvenirs

Food to Try

Fresh seafood, traditional Moroccan pastries, mint tea

Exploration Time

2-3 hours for a complete experience

Tip

Bargaining is expected in shops - start at 50% of asking price

Corniche of Casablanca

Corniche

The Corniche is Casablanca's seaside promenade stretching along the Atlantic coast. This vibrant area features beaches, swimming pools, restaurants, and cafes. It's the perfect place for a stroll, to watch the sunset, or to enjoy fresh seafood with ocean views.

Best Time to Visit

Late afternoon for sunset views or weekends for lively atmosphere

Dining Options

Seafood restaurants, beach clubs, cafes with ocean views

Activities

Swimming, beach walks, people watching, dining

Tip

Try the fresh grilled fish at one of the seaside restaurants

Habous Quarter

Habous Quarter

The Habous Quarter, also known as the "New Medina," was built by the French in the 1930s. It features beautiful architecture blending French and Moroccan styles, with wide streets, arches, and traditional souks. This area is known for its artisanal workshops and high-quality crafts.

Crafts Available

Leather goods, traditional clothing, metalwork, pottery, books

Notable Buildings

Mahkama du Pacha (court building), Royal Palace gates

Photo Spots

Beautiful architecture, colorful doors, traditional fountains

Tip

Visit the traditional bakeries for fresh Moroccan bread

Mohammed V Square

Mohammed V Square

Mohammed V Square is the administrative center of Casablanca, surrounded by important government buildings in Mauresque architecture (a blend of Moroccan and French styles). The square features beautiful fountains, gardens, and is often filled with pigeons. It's the heart of the modern city.

Surrounding Buildings

Wilaya (Prefecture), Palace of Justice, Central Post Office, Bank of Morocco

Best Time to Visit

Morning or late afternoon when the fountains are illuminated

Features

Large fountains, gardens, pigeons, impressive architecture

Tip

Combine your visit with nearby United Nations Square and Arab League Park

Day Trip to Rabat - Places to Visit

Rabat, Morocco's capital, offers a blend of historical sites and modern attractions. Here are the top places to visit:

Hassan Tower

Unfinished minaret of what would have been the world's largest mosque, dating back to the 12th century.

Kasbah of the Udayas

Beautiful blue and white fortress overlooking the Atlantic Ocean, with stunning gardens and narrow streets.

Chellah Necropolis

Ancient Roman and Islamic ruins with gardens, storks' nests, and historical monuments.

Royal Palace

Official residence of the King of Morocco, with impressive architecture and beautiful grounds.

Day Trip to Marrakech - Places to Visit

Marrakech, the "Red City," is famous for its vibrant culture, historical sites, and bustling markets. Here are the top places to visit:

Jemaa el-Fnaa Square

Famous main square with food stalls, musicians, storytellers, and snake charmers.

Koutoubia Mosque

Largest mosque in Marrakech with a stunning 77-meter minaret, built in the 12th century.

Bahia Palace

19th-century palace with beautiful gardens, courtyards, and intricate Moroccan architecture.

Majorelle Garden

Vibrant botanical garden created by French painter Jacques Majorelle, with a striking blue villa.
